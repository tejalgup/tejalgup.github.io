When I was a second-year, I received an email from the Economics department at Pitt with an advertisement. There was an opportunity to join an Economics professor for research and they wanted somebody with a strong computer science background. Not to be the person to put myself down but, I definitely was not and still do not consider myself an expert in computer science. But I knew I had to start putting myself out there and applied anyways; one of the best decisions I've made in my undergraduate education thus far.

I got the chance to talk with [Dr. Allison Shertzer](https://www.allisonshertzer.com/) pretty quickly after and luckily for me she was impressed with my background. I started doing trainings and learning about maps and editing. 

Then, we quickly ran into Murphy's Law. For those of you that don't know what it is: "Anything that can go wrong will go wrong."


This is about my economic research under Dr. Allison Shertzer. I write about map editing here and ARC GIS.
