**When Love Strikes**

I looked to the stars; another night but it's still you I see.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

“Ugh, the weather is starting to get bad Nori. We should hurry up,” my roommate Mona calls out to me as we pack our beach towels and hurry towards the car. The weather was so perfect on the mainland, but a dark front was approaching from oceanside. It slowly begins to drizzle over us as we put up our umbrellas.

Suddenly, a white light blinds me. As the light fades, I find myself transported to nighttime, and my legs are moving faster than I remembered. I’m running so fast that I can’t keep up with it and topple over face planting into the cold sand. Wait, that can’t be right? It was around 2 pm the last time I checked. I look around and see the familiar Santa Cruz Boardwalk Amusement Park lit up in the distance. I hear the sound of footsteps trailing me, somebody’s following me. I try to look around to figure out who it is, but they’re engulfed in the darkness still. “What’s going on?” I call out.

“Who are you?” I hear a husky voice call out.

And once again, a white light takes over my vision.

“Nori!” I hear Mona yell. I open my eyes with my face still down. My left arm- no, my entire left side was throbbing in pain.

“Mona!” I yell back as Mona runs over to me and helps me off my feet. “Ugh, what happened?” I squeak out as I get my footing.

“You were struck by LIGHTNING!” Mona exclaims.

That can’t be true, can it? “But I’m not on fire? My skin isn’t burnt off. But my arm hurts like hell,” I reply

“Well, it hit your umbrella and traveled through you presumably,” she says. I can see the fear in her eye.

“Let’s get out of here asap,” I reply as we run towards her car.

Luckily, we were able to make it incident-free back safe in her Jeep, my whole-body aching. We were panting heavily trying to catch our breath. The shock of what happened began to wash over me. “Mona, the craziest thing happened-“

She cuts me off “Girl, I know. I was there. Are you okay?” still clearly worried.

“Yeah, no I’m fine. Well, it hurts, but I’m okay. But not that. When it hit me, I had this almost vision sort of,” I try to explain.

“It was probably like some sort of dream or your life flashing before your eyes. That happens when traumatic things happen to people. Hey, do you think we should go to the hospital?” Mona explained. She could be right; I did get hit by lightning! I was probably just out of it and my mind played tricks on me, so I thought I saw God or something.

I shake my head, “Yeah, that probably wasn’t too good for my heart. I should get checked out just in case,” I instruct as Mona starts the car and begins to drive. I grab the aux slowly (still in a ton of pain) and turn on some K-Pop. I feel tears well up in my eyes. “I can’t believe that just happened, how unlucky can one person be?”

“Ironic for a statistician,” Mona chuckles. 

I had just graduated college and moved all the way from Chicago to San Jose to work as a Statistical Modeler; it was a well-needed change. Having just been through a break-up, I desperately needed a change of pace. I was ready for my life to be new, completely turn around and enjoy being single so I could stop being consistently let down and dumped by men who don’t even like me. Okay- if you can’t tell, I might not be completely over things. 

It was the first time being truly on my own, but luckily enough I had already found Mona. My company had an online forum for new employees, and she had a group put together and needed a fourth person which I happily obliged. So far, things have been perfect, but I couldn’t help but feel a little homesick. Our other two roommates, Stella and Penelope, were busy at a San Jose Sharks game. We had plans to meet up for dinner, but that might be delayed now. 

“I prefer being called a model, thank you very much,” I say weakly while giving her a smile. “I’m going to text Stella and Penny and let them know what happened. Do you think they’ll believe me lol?” I say as I start texting our group chat.

“Oh my god, you did not just say ‘LOL’ out loud. You’re more valley girl than me, and I’m actually FROM the Valley,” she laughs trying to cheer me up. Mona was one of those people that could just read others. Maybe I’m especially easy to read, but it just makes our connection that much easier.

“That is not a Valley girl thing, you’re just trying to paint me in a bad light. Ugh, I miss home. Cali is amazing, it really is, but I can’t help but miss my mom,” I express.
Mona nods her head, “Hey, I get it. Being away from family for the first time is really hard. But you’re not alone. And this is the chance you’ve always wanted. You’ve just got to give it all you have. Why not try getting some rest? I’ll wake you up when we’re at the hospital.”

“Yeah, thanks Mon,” as I give her a genuine smile. This is what I’ve always wanted. I rest my eyes and let myself drift.
